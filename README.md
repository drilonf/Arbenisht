# Introduction
ArbГ«nisht (ArbЙӣЖһisht) is a language reform proposal for the Albanian language. It aims to produce a standard language which is etymological and pan-dialectal.
<br>

The current standard language is largely based on Northern Tosk, and as a result excludes many Gheg and Southern Tosk features, even when these features are more conservative. This proposed standard aims to not be biased towards any one dialect (besides to an extent Elbasanisht) but rather favours forms that have been used by Old Albanian writers cross-dialectally for hundreds of years. Conservative forms are chosen regardless of which dialect they are preserved in today. Features lost in all dialects are not re-instituted. <br>

In cases where competing Old Albanian forms of words exist, then the form used by Elbasanisht will be favoured. Elbasanisht is a Southern Gheg dialect with Northern Tosk influence, and as a result is understandable by virtually all Albanian speakers, even without prior contact to the dialect. This is why literary Elbasanisht was chosen by intellectuals, cross-dialectally, to be the neutral Standard language of Albania during the period of the 1900s-1940s - until the Communist takeover replaced this with literary Northern Tosk. Additionally, the phonetics of Elbasanisht are favoured for the Standard pronunciation. <br>

With that said, this reform has been designed with the principle of "one spellings, many pronunciations" - meaning that it aims to encourage speakers to write uniformly but pronunce words as they would in their own dialect. This is a similar approach to Nynorsk for Norwegian. Such a pan-dialectal language reform would be able to better integrate Gheg & Southern Tosk speakers (including the ArbГ«resh of Greece & Italy) without eroding their dialects.

# Spelling Changes

## _Г«_ replaced by _Йӣ_
This reform proposes replacing _Г«_ with _Йӣ_ so as to eliminate a diacritic letter, which clutter orthographies, so as to assign each sound a unique letter (or digraph) without diacritics. _Йӣ_ was chosen as I believe it's the best aesthetic alternative to _Г«_; it has some historical basis, having been previously used by the Stamboll Alphabet; it is quicker to handwrite; and it is arguably more distinctive from _e_ than _Г«_ is. Additionally, since _Г«_ is such a commonly occuring letter, its replacement with _Йӣ_ would quickly signify to the user that the new spelling standard is being used, even in short text.

Note - unlike the rest of the proposal, this is simply an stylistic choice and is not an important part of the proposal. _Г«_ continuing to be used instead would not hinder the proposal at all.

## _Г§_ replaced by _ch_

This reform proposes replacing _Г§_ with _ch_. This matches with the existing _-h_ pattern of digraphs in Albanian, such as for: _d_ вҶ’ _dh_, _s_ вҶ’ _sh_, _t_ вҶ’ _th_, _x_ вҶ’ _xh_, _z_ вҶ’ _zh_. So, _c_ вҶ’ _ch_ logically follows, moreso than the existing _c_ вҶ’ _Г§_. Additionally, this eliminates a diacritic letter, which is beneficial as _Г§_ is frequently incorrectly typed as _c_.
*	_**Г§**farГ«_ (~_**Г§**ar_) вҶ’ _**ch**farЙӣ_
*	_**Г§**aj_ вҶ’ _**ch**aj_
*	_**Г§**liroj_ вҶ’ _**ch**liroj_
*	_di**Г§**ka_ вҶ’ _di**ch**ka_
*	_gjith**Г§**ka_ вҶ’ _gjithЙӣ**ch**ka_

## _И·_ introduced

In some cases, Proto-Albanian and Old Albanian medial /l/ evolved via a historical /КҺ/ intermediary to /j/. However, some Gheg & Tosk dialects retained the original /l/ or intermediary /КҺ/. In these cases, Standard _j_ is replaced by _И·_, which can be pronounced /j/, /l/, or /КҺ/, depending on the dialect.
*	_u**j**k_ (\~_u**l**k_\~_uk_) вҶ’ _u**И·**k_
*	_go**j**Г«_ (\~_go**l**Г«_) вҶ’ _go**И·**Йӣ_
*	_bi**j**Г«_ (\~_bi**l**Г«_\~_bi**lj**Г«_) вҶ’ _bi**И·**Йӣ_

This historic /КҺ/ is theorised by Dedvukaj & Ndoci (2023) to be the precursor of the _l_ in Old Albanian _gl_ and _kl_. Proto-Albanian /gКҺ/ and /kКҺ/ eventually developed to modern _gj_ and _q_ in most dialects, _g(i)_ and _k(i)_ in Northern Gheg, and _gl(j)_ and _kl(j)_ in Southern Tosk. This is discussed further below.

Note - this respelling does not include cases where some dialects have /КҺ/ in place of Standard /l/. In these cases, /КҺ/ is considered an alternative pronunciation of _l_ and is not marked. e.g. _lule_ is /lulЙӣ/ in most Gheg & Tosk dialects, but /КҺuКҺЙӣ/ in some Northwestern Gheg and Southern Tosk dialects.

## _gj_ replaced by _gj_, _gИ·_

Old Gheg & Old Tosk _gl_ is is preserved as _gl\~glj_ /gl\~gКҺ/ in some Tosk dialects, realised as _gi\~g_ /gКІ\~g/ in some Gheg dialects, and merged with _gj_ /dК‘/ (and similar) in the rest. Standard Albanian also merges this sound with _gj_. This reform proposes replacing these cases, where _gj_ descends from _gl_, with _gИ·_, which can be pronounced as /Йҹ\~dК‘\~ЙҹКқ\~dК’/, /gКІ\~g/, or /gl\~gКҺ/, depending on the dialect.

* _**gj**eth_ вҶ’ _**gj**eth_
* _**gj**erГ«_ (\~_**gj**ГўnГ«_\~_**gj**Г«rГ«_) вҶ’ _**gj**aЖһЙӣ_
* _**gj**ak_ вҶ’ _**gj**ak_
* _**gj**i_ (\~_**gj**Г®_) вҶ’ _**gj**i_
* _**gj**u_ (\~_**g**Г»_\~_**gi**Г»_\~_**gl**u_) вҶ’ _**gИ·**u_
* _**gj**atГ«_ (\~_**g**ГўtГ«_\~_**g**ГҙtГ«_\~_**gl**atГ«_) вҶ’ _**gИ·**atЙӣ_
* _**gj**uhГ«_ (\~_**g**uhГ«_\~_**gi**uhГ«_\~_**gl**uhГ«_\~_**glj**uhГ«_) вҶ’ _**gИ·**uhЙӣ_

## _q_ replaced by _kj_, _kИ·_

This reform proposes replacing _q_ with _kj_. This matches with the existing _-j_ pattern to mark palatal consonants: _g_ вҶ’ _gj_, _n_ вҶ’ _nj_. So, _k_ вҶ’ _kj_ logically follows, moreso than the existing _k_ вҶ’ _q_. _kj_ also matches with historical spellings of this phoneme (_ci_, _chi_, _ki_, _kj_ in Old Albanian).<br>
Old Gheg & Old Tosk _kl_ is preserved as _kl\~klj_ /kl\~kКҺ/ in some Tosk dialects, realised as _ki\~k_ /kКІ\~k/ in some Gheg dialects, and merged with _q_ /tЙ•/ (and similar) in the rest. Standard Albanian also merges this sound with _q_. This reform proposes replacing these cases, where _q_ descends from _kl_, with _kИ·_, which can be pronounced as /c\~tЙ•\~cГ§\~tКғ/, /kКІ\~k/, or /kl\~kКҺ/, depending on the dialect.
*	_**q**en_ вҶ’ _**kj**en_
*	_pesh**q**it_ вҶ’ _pesh**kj**it_
*	_**q**aj_ (\~_**k**aj_\~_**ki**aj_\~_**q**anj_\~_**kl**anj_) вҶ’ _**kИ·**anj_
*	_**q**enГ«_ (\~_**k**ГўnГ«_\~_**k**ГӘnГ«_\~_**q**ГўnГ«_\~_**q**Г«nГ«_\~_**kl**Г«nГ«_) вҶ’ _**kИ·**anЙӣ_
*	_**q**ep_ (\~_**k**ep_\~_**kl**ep_) вҶ’ _**kИ·**ep_

There are currently a few words in Albanian containing _kj_ /k.j/. These would be changed to _ki_ to avoid confusion with the _kj_ digraph.
* _fi**kj**e_ вҶ’ _fi**ki**e_
* _vde**kj**a_ вҶ’ _vde**ki**a_

A similar change was made in Standard Albanian previously for _gj_, where words like _dje**gj**e_ were respelled _dje**gi**e_ to avoid confusion with the _gj_ digraph.

## _Жһ_ introduced

Tosk _r_ often corresponds to Gheg _n_ medially and finally. The Gheg form is more conservative, observable from Latin loans; although, Tosk rhoticisation is very old, predating written works. In these cases, _Жһ_ will be used instead of Standard _r_. This new letter helps unify the dialects, and often helps mark Gheg nasalisation.
*	_a**r**mik_ (\~_Гў**n**mik_) вҶ’ _a**Жһ**Йӣmik_
*	_emГ«**r**_ (\~_ГӘmГ«**n**_) вҶ’ _emЙӣ**Жһ**_

This also applies to historical _n_ where Gheg dropped the sound entirely but Tosk retained a rhoticised form.
*	_krye**r**_ (\~_krye_) вҶ’ _krye**Жһ**_
*	_pa**r**Г«_ (\~_pa_) вҶ’ _pa**Жһ**Йӣ_

## Syllable-final _j_ often replaced by _nj_

Old Gheg & Old Tosk both used medial & final _nj_ in verbs. Gheg and a few Tosk dialects dropped this, but most other Tosk dialects retain this. Also, this often helps mark Gheg nasalisation.
*	_nda**j**_ (\~_dГў**j**_\~_nda**nj**_) вҶ’ _da**nj**_
*	_gje**j**_ (\~_gjГӘ**j**_\~_gjГ«**nj**_) вҶ’ _gje**nj**_
*	_mbi**j**_ (\~_mbГ®**j**_\~_mbi**nj**_) вҶ’ _mbi**nj**_
*	_shko**j**_ (\~_shko**nj**_) вҶ’ _shko**nj**_

This has already been preserved in some Standard words (that aren't verbs).
*	_mГ«dhe**nj**_ (\~_mГ«dhГӘ**j**_) вҶ’ _mЙӣdhe**nj**_
*	_bri**nj**Г«_ (\~_brГ®**j**Г«_) вҶ’ _bri**nj**Йӣ_

## Most _ua_ replaced by _uo_

Modern _ua_ often corresponds to Old Gheg _uo_ and Old Tosk _ua_. Both are theorised to descend from Proto-Albanian *_uo_ вҶҗ *_ЕҚ_. Although _uo_ is largely extinct today, in this proposal it serves to unify Gheg _ue_, _Е«_, and Tosk _ua_. Standard Albanian currently uses both Tosk (_ua_, _uar_, _or)_ and Gheg (_ues_) forms.
*	_m**ua**_ (\~_m**ue**_\~_m**Е«**_) вҶ’ _m**uo**_
*	_mГ«s**ua**r_ (\~_mГ«s**ue**_\~_mГ«s**Е«**_) вҶ’ _mЙӣs**uo**_
*	_mГ«s**ue**s_ (\~_mГ«s**Е«**s_\~_mГ«s**ua**s_\~_mГ«sonjГ«s_) вҶ’ _mЙӣs**uo**s_
*	_fjal**o**r_ (\~_fjal**ue**r_\~_fjal**Е«**r_) вҶ’ _fjal**uo**r_

## Nasal pronunciations introduced

Gheg nasals are likely more conservative than Tosk's oral equivalents. Literary GhegвҖҷs 5 nasal vowels (_Гў_/_Гҙ_, _ГӘ_, _Г®_, _Г»_, _Е·_) are adopted without diacritics (_a_, _e_, _i_, _u_, _y_). Nasal forms in this proposal are not written but implied by appearing before _m_, _n_, _Жһ_, _nj_, and word-finally. Gheg nasal vowels correspond to Tosk oral (and sometimes reduced) vowels.
*	_**a**nГ«_ (\~_**Гў**nГ«_) вҶ’ _**a**nЙӣ_
*	_v**e**nd_ (\~_v**ГӘ**nd_\~_v**Г«**nd_) вҶ’ _v**e**nd_
*	_br**e**j_ (\~_br**ГӘ**j_\~_br**e**nj_) вҶ’ _br**e**nj_
*	_k**Г«**mbГ«_ (\~_k**Гў**mbГ«_\~_k**Гҙ**mbГ«_) вҶ’ _k**a**mbЙӣ_
*	_br**i**_ (\~_br**Г®**_) вҶ’ _br**i**_
*	_h**u**ndГ«_ (\~_h**Г»**ndГ«_) вҶ’ _h**u**ndЙӣ_

A more comprehensive vowel system could have been implemented, by implementing one or more of the following:
* Marking all Gheg nasals with _Гў_, _ГӘ_, _Г®_, _Г»_, _Е·_, or _ГЈ_, _бәҪ_, _Д©_, _Е©_, _б»№_
* Marking Gheg nasals vowels that reduce to schwa in Tosk with _Дғ_, _Д•_
* Marking long Gheg vowels with _ДҒ_, _Д“_, _Д«_, _ЕҚ_, _Е«_, _Иі_

However, these were avoided so as to not overcomplicate the orthography with diacritics.

Additionally, nasalisation is not consistent across Gheg dialects, and so marking nasal vowels would bias certain Gheg subdialects over others. The previously described markers of nasalisation (and therefore of Tosk vowel reduction) should be enough for readers to figure out the pronunciation of words in their dialect.

# Spelling Retentions

## Syllable-final _h_ preserved

Old Gheg and Old Tosk both used syllable-final _h_. This changed to _f_ in Gheg but not in Tosk.
*	_fto**h**_ (\~_fto**f**_) вҶ’ _fto**h**_
*	_ngro**h**_ (\~_ngro**f**_) вҶ’ _ngro**h**_
*	_njo**h**_ (\~_njo**f**_) вҶ’ _njo**h**_
*	_sho**h**_ (\~_sho**f**_) вҶ’ _sho**h**_

## _mb_, _nd_ clusters preserved

Old Gheg and Old Tosk both used _mb_, _nd_ clusters. These simplified to _m_, _n_ in Gheg but not in Tosk.
*	_**mb**ush_ (\~_**mbМҶ**ush_) вҶ’ _**mb**ush_
*	_**nd**er_ (\~_**ndМҶ**ГӘr_) вҶ’ _**nd**er_

Literary Gheg has already adopted this.
## Word-final _Йӣ_ preserved

Old Gheg and Old Tosk both used _Г«_ word-finally. These were dropped in Gheg but not in Tosk. _Г«_ is still useful for Gheg as it indicates noun gender, conjugations & declensions, and lengthening of the penultimate vowel.
*	_shum**Г«**_ (\~_shum**eМҲМҶ**_) вҶ’ _shum**Йӣ**_
*	_burr**Г«**_ (\~_burr**eМҲМҶ**_) вҶ’ _burr**Йӣ**_

Literary Gheg has already adopted this.

# Suggestions for typing
In regards to keyboard typing, it is proposed that _W_ is replaced with _Жҗ_ on Albanian keyboards, as _W_ is not used in Albanian, besides in recent unassimilated loanwords, whilst _Жҗ_ is extremely common. Additional characters could then be produced by pressing Alt and selecting _Vv_ (for _Ww_), _Jj_ (for _ЙҲИ·_), or _Nn_ (for _РҳЖһ_). Alternatively, and for mobiles, _W_, _ЙҲ_, and _Рҳ_ can be placed at the top-right corner of the keyboard, as Albanian currently does (for _ГҮ_ and _ГӢ_), and many other languages currently do for their own non-standard characters.
